    Tips from expert on body shapes, sizes, and skin complexions

    Nonye Goodie-Obi, the Creative Director and CEO of Nonnistics, offered valuable insights to designers on crafting lace styles for women in an interview with Legit.ng. According to Nonye, understanding the five distinct body shapes—pear, apple, inverted triangle, perfect eight, and I-shape—is crucial for creating tailored and flattering designs. She said:

    For clarity, a pear-shaped woman is smaller up and bulkier down. An apple-shaped woman is rotund. An inverted triangle-shaped woman is bulkier p (broad-shouldered) and narrower down. A perfect eight-woman is evenly proportioned. An I-shaped woman is straight without curves. Designing for each of these shapes, requires understanding of the flawed area so as to balance it out.

    Nonye presented tailored styles and designs for women based on their shapes and sizes, offering specific advice:

    • For the inverted-triangle shape: Peplums on the waist, gowns that flare off the hip, ball gowns, dirndl skirts, and A-line dresses are recommended.
    • For the pear shape: Focus on widening the shoulders to balance with the hips, utilizing exaggerated sleeves, shoulder pads, and motifs on the shoulder.
    • For the apple shape: Opt for a boat neck cut and diagonal ruches, avoiding high necklines.
    • For the perfect eight shape: Any style is suitable.
    • For the I-shape: Consider a padded bust and hip for a perfect eight-figure, but avoid bodycon dresses.

    The creative director emphasized that plus-size women, particularly those with apple-shaped and inverted-triangle body types, should avoid being confined in fully boned corsets. Additionally, she stressed the importance of considering skin tones, advising against using bright intensities of colors that match one's complexion.

    Fashion trends are recycled. Remember, fashion is dynamic, so staying informed through a combination of these methods is crucial.
