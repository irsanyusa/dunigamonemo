    We live in a world where computers are a must. They help people work faster and more efficiently. Computer technology is a constantly developing field. Although people have managed to achieve a lot via computers, there is still a lot of space for further improvement and invention. There will always be a high demand for experts in the IT world. Why not study computer science and IT in the Czech Republic? There are some great options. Check them out.

    Faculty of Mathematics and Physics, Charles University in Prague

    Charles University has a very long tradition dating back to the 14th century. The Faculty of Mathematics and Physics has a great reputation. The students get wonderful opportunities to try out their skills in the commercial sector during their studies. The graduates can expect a bright future. They are well prepared to work in prominent and successful companies. The school is located in the center of Prague.

    Faculty of Informatics and Statistics, Prague University of Economics and Business

    This school offers programs such as Applied Informatics or Information Systems Management. The students are provided with plenty of useful skills to start a promising career. It’s a great investment for the future. It’s possible to study Master’s or Doctoral programs in English. The Faculty of Informatics and Statistics is a very important center for statistics, econometrics, or IT management.

    Faculty of Science, University of South Bohemia in České Budějovice

    AI is constantly developing and it’s exciting to think about the possibilities it will bring to people in the future. What about studying Artificial Intelligence and Data Science at the Faculty of Science of the University of South Bohemia? This university is a great choice. It’s an important research center.

    Anglo-American University

    Anglo-American University is the oldest private university in the Czech Republic. It offers many interesting programs, such as Computer Science or Computer Information Systems. Students from all around the world study there. The approach to each student is individual and the main focus is on attaining practical skills. The school is situated in the Lesser Town of Prague, which is a very pleasant location in the historical center of Czechia’s capital city.

    Unicorn University

    Unicorn University in Prague is another great option. At this private school, you can study Software Development. The school is oriented toward gaining experience important to succeed in the job market. The students get involved in real projects, which is a big plus. Their instructors are professionals in software development and management.

    To find out more about studying computer science and technology in the Czech Republic, click here. Good luck choosing the best school for you!
