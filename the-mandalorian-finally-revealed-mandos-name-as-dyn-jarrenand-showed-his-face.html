    One of the biggest mysteries of the entire first season of The Mandalorian has surrounded the titular character's identitywho he is, where he's from, what he stands for; most importantly, maybe, who's even under that helmet. In the show's first season finale, all of that was finally answered, as viewers learned a bit about Mando's history, his real name ( Dyn Jarren , as Pedro Pascal previously teased in a video ), and finally an in-universe look at his face underneath his helmet.

    Early in the episode, Moff Gideon makes clear that he knows exactly who's being held up inside the cave from the end of the previous episode. There's bounty hunter guild leader Greef Carga (Carl Weathers), deserter shock trooper Cara Dune (Gina Carano), and The Mandalorian, revealed to be named Dyn Jarren. By knowing his name, Dyn reveals to his companions, that means that Gideon was involved with the Mandalorian purge. A Mandalorian isn't a race , necessarily, but a creed . When Dyn's parents were killed, The Mandalorians took him in, and his name has been kept on the down low ever since.

    Dyn/Mando is badly injured in the battle with Moff Gideon, and seems resigned to succumb to his wounds. However, when he tells IG-11 that no living being has seen his face since he was a child, IG-11 reminds him: he is not living. He is a droid. IG-11 lifts his helmet off without any more debate, and for the first time we see Pascal's familiar face . The Game of Thrones and upcoming Wonder Woman 1984 star looks pretty much how he always looks, but it's the first canonical look at the character underneath his mask.

    In the end, IG-11 was able to save Dyn, and allow him to continue his journeywhich is already being set up strongly for season 2. Directed by Taika Waititi, the finale may have been the best episode of The Mandalorian yet, and with a ton more information now known about our hero, it should help to make season 2whenever it may comeeven stronger.
